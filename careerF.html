Tips and Tricks for Career Fairs

  • Dress Appropriately! Never judge a book by its cover gets thrown out the window during a Career Fair. Recruiters want to see that you care about making a good first impression.
  • Get your resumes. Print off at least 10 -15 copies of your resume.
  • Use. Your. Resources! Have either a friend, family member or Career Services look over your resume. Use spell check to make sure you remembered the e in collge.
  • Relax. Recruiters are people too. You are trying to find a job and they are trying to find candidates. The more relaxed you are the easier it is to talk to them.

Mara Howard

Lewis Clark State College 2016

"Career Fairs are a process and if you’re prepared, you’ll succeed. Figure out who’s going to be there and go in with a plan. Do your homework by looking at the company website. Look at their values, mission statements, creeds, everything. By doing this you gain background information about the company and can have an intelligent conversation with the recruiters. You’ll start off with an advantage over other students."

Jenna Mayer

University of Idaho 2020

"When I attend Career Fairs, I do a walk-through of the fair first. I start by walking right down the middle and try not to get the recruiters attention. As I’m doing this, I’m look for companies that are familiar, but I also look for unique firms as well. After I finish looking, I go through again with my resume and stop at the ones I’ve made notes of. When that is over, I go through again for a third time to make sure I talked to everyone I wanted to."

Referring to the tips and tricks from above, here are some resources for you to use! The University of Idaho’s Career Services is amazing for looking at your cover letters and giving advice on elevator pitches. Another resource is the State University of New York, Binghamton University (SUNY Bing). Binghamton University is a renowned public university and their Resume Builder has helped me create my professional resume. The pointers that show up throughout their worksheets are valuable and apply to almost every situation.
